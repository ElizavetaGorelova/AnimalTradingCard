Seal

Seals are part of the pinniped order of marine mammals, which also includes sea lions, walruses and fur seals.

  • Scientific name: Pinniped.
  • Body length: About 15-20 feet long.
  • Average life: 20 years.
  • Habitat: Seals typically live in the cold ocean waters of the Arctic or off the coasts of Antarctica.

While there are many differences among the species, all seals have feet shaped like fins. In fact, the word pinniped means "fin-footed" in Latin. Those fin-shaped feet make them supreme swimmers, and all pinnipeds are considered semi-aquatic marine mammals.
